Some writing

“Why is it, then, that apparitions are not so prevalent after the invention of the camera, he says?

Why… it’s much earlier than that. They began to fade when humanity lost its faith. When we ceased to see because we ceased to believe. Perhaps the apparitions were never for the faithful, but for those who could not feel the divine unless it forced itself upon their senses.

Ever since we chose to walk apart from God — tracing our own routes, worshiping our own inventions, the fragile idols of human hands — the heavens grew silent. We replaced the Creator with the creature, truth with spectacle, grace with machinery. And so we no longer behold miracles, not because they vanished, but because we built a world where they cannot be recognized.

We demand proof from lenses and sensors, yet deny the soul its witness. The sacred withdrew not out of malice, but out of mercy — for how could light dwell among those who prefer the glow of their own screens to the radiance of the Word?”
